# Air Quality Levels

Nexus uses the following air quality categories to help you quickly understand air quality readings across the platform. The same categories appear on the dashboard, map, dataset visualizer, and data export pages.

---

## Categories and Ranges

| Level | Color | PM₂.₅ Range (µg/m³) | PM₁₀ Range (µg/m³) | What It Means |
|-------|--------|---------------------|--------------------|---------------|
| **Good** | Green | 0 – 9.0 | 0 – 54.0 | Air quality is satisfactory. |
| **Moderate** | Yellow / Amber | 9.1 – 35.4 | 54.1 – 154.0 | Acceptable for most people. |
| **Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups** | Orange | 35.5 – 55.4 | 154.1 – 254.0 | Sensitive people should limit prolonged outdoor exertion. |
| **Unhealthy** | Red | 55.5 – 125.4 | 254.1 – 354.0 | Everyone should reduce prolonged outdoor exertion. |
| **Very Unhealthy** | Purple | 125.5 – 225.4 | 354.1 – 424.0 | Health warnings — avoid prolonged outdoor exertion. |
| **Hazardous** | Pink / Magenta | 225.5+ | 424.1+ | Health alert — avoid all outdoor activity. |

:::note
These ranges are specific to AirQo's categorization. They may differ from air quality indexes used by other organizations.
:::

---

## Where Levels Appear

You will see these levels in several places:

- **Nexus Dashboard** — on location cards and chart legends
- **Interactive Map** — as marker colors and icons, and in the map legend
- **Dataset Visualizer** — on map charts and reference lines
- **Location Details Panel** — in the Current Air Quality card and forecast cards
